Maintenance Note — Pump P-101 (Boiler Feed Water Pump, Unit 1)

Date: 2026-06-18
Author: Rotating Equipment Team
Asset: P-101 (centrifugal boiler feed water pump)
Area: Demo Plant — Utilities

Summary
-------
Pump P-101 has been running with elevated vibration over the past two weeks.
Operators reported increased noise from the drive end during the last shift,
and the control room logged an overall vibration reading of 7.8 mm/s RMS on
the outboard bearing, up from a baseline of 2.9 mm/s RMS recorded after the
last overhaul. The pump remains in service but is being monitored closely.

Observations
------------
- High vibration concentrated at the drive-end (outboard) bearing housing.
- Vibration spectrum shows a dominant 1x running-speed peak with rising
  harmonics, consistent with shaft misalignment between the pump and motor.
- Early signs of bearing wear: a low-amplitude high-frequency component was
  detected on the outboard bearing, suggesting degrading rolling elements.
- Bearing housing temperature trending upward (68 C vs. 54 C baseline).
- No abnormal seal leakage observed since the recent maintenance work.

Recent Maintenance History
---------------------------
- 2026-06-05: Mechanical seal replaced on P-101 following minor process-side
  weep. Coupling was disturbed during the seal change. Laser alignment was
  noted as "to be re-verified" but the follow-up check was not recorded.

Probable Cause
--------------
The vibration signature (dominant 1x peak, rising temperature, recent coupling
disturbance) points to shaft misalignment introduced during the 2026-06-05
mechanical seal replacement. Continued operation in a misaligned condition is
accelerating bearing wear on the drive end.

Inspection Recommendation
-------------------------
1. Perform a laser shaft alignment check between P-101 pump and motor and
   correct to within tolerance.
2. Inspect and trend the outboard (drive-end) bearing; replace if wear
   indicators confirm degradation.
3. Re-baseline vibration after alignment correction and confirm the 1x peak
   has dropped.
4. Update the maintenance record to close out the deferred alignment
   verification from the seal replacement job.

Priority: HIGH — risk of bearing failure if misalignment is not corrected.
